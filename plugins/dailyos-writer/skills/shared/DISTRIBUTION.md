## Channel Characteristics

### Slack

**Format constraints**:
- 150 words max (most will skim)
- Lead with the takeaway
- One key point, not three
- No markdown headers (use **bold** and line breaks)
- Emoji for visual breaks, not decoration

**Structure**:
```
[One-line summary/hook]

[2-3 sentences of context or detail]

[Next step or link if needed]
```

### LinkedIn

**Format constraints**:
- 150-300 words (engagement drops after)
- Hook in first line (visible before "see more")
- Line breaks between paragraphs
- Personal perspective, not corporate voice
- Question or call-to-action at end

**Structure**:
```
[Hook - the scroll-stopping line]

[Context - why this matters]

[Insight - what you learned/observed]

[Takeaway - what others can do with this]

[Question or engagement prompt]
```

**What to sanitize**:
- Customer names â†’ "enterprise customer" or industry
- Specific revenue â†’ ranges or "significant"
- Internal project names â†’ generic descriptions
- Proprietary strategies â†’ general patterns

**Example transformation** (Internal insight â†’ LinkedIn):

### Personal Blog

**Format characteristics**:
- 1200-2500 words (depth expected)
- More personal voice than P2
- Can include opinion and speculation
- Readers opt in (lower urgency)
- SEO matters if public

**What to keep from internal**:
- Core insights and frameworks
- General patterns and observations
- Personal stories (with discretion)
- Industry analysis

**What to remove**:
- Customer-identifying details
- Internal metrics and ARR
- Competitive positioning specifics
- Confidential strategies

**What to add**:
- More personal context
- Broader industry framing
- External examples and references
- Calls to action (comments, newsletter, etc.)

---

### Executive Email

**Format constraints**:
- 200-400 words max
- BLUF mandatory (conclusion in first sentence)
- One ask per email
- Mobile-readable (short paragraphs)
- Bold the key information

**Structure**:
```
Subject: [Action needed]: [Topic]

[First sentence: The ask or key point]

[2-3 short paragraphs: Context and support]

[Clear next step with timeline]
```

## Repurposing Workflows

### Internal â†’ External (Sanitization)

Content created for P2s, internal updates, or customer-specific docs can often be repurposed for broader distribution with proper sanitization.

**Step 1: Content audit**
- [ ] What's confidential? (customer names, revenue, strategy)
- [ ] What's proprietary? (competitive positioning, pricing)
- [ ] What's generalizable? (patterns, frameworks, insights)

**Step 2: Generalize specifics**

| Internal | External |
|----------|----------|
| "Cox Automotive" | "A Fortune 500 automotive company" |
| "$400K deal" | "Six-figure partnership" |
| "Summit ring customer" | "Strategic account" |
| "Our Agentforce connector" | "AI integration capabilities" |
| "Beating Contentful" | "Competing effectively in enterprise evaluations" |

**Step 3: Reframe perspective**

Internal content often assumes shared context. External content needs:
- Industry framing (why this matters broadly)
- Personal angle (your perspective, not company position)
- Teaching orientation (what others can learn)

**Step 4: Channel adaptation**

Apply format guidelines for target channel.

---

### Long â†’ Short (Compression)

For repurposing long-form content into shorter formats.

**Compression levels**:

| From | To | Keep |
|------|----|----- |
| 1500-word P2 | 300-word LinkedIn | One insight + one example |
| 1500-word P2 | 100-word Slack | The headline + the "so what" |
| 1500-word P2 | 400-word email | BLUF + key evidence + ask |

**Compression technique**:
1. State the main insight in one sentence
2. Find the single best supporting evidence
3. Cut everything else
4. Add appropriate framing for channel

---

### Series â†’ Synthesis

Multiple related pieces can be synthesized into new formats.

**Example**: Weekly impact captures â†’ Monthly summary â†’ Quarterly narrative

**Synthesis technique**:
1. Identify themes across pieces
2. Extract the "so what" pattern
3. Find the story that connects them
4. Write fresh synthesis (not just compilation)

## Sanitization Checklist

Before publishing externally, verify:

**Customer confidentiality**:
- [ ] No customer names without approval
- [ ] No specific revenue figures
- [ ] No internal project names
- [ ] No stakeholder names

**Competitive sensitivity**:
- [ ] No specific competitor disparagement
- [ ] No win/loss details that identify accounts
- [ ] No pricing or commercial terms
- [ ] No unpublished product roadmap

**Strategic sensitivity**:
- [ ] No internal strategy documents referenced
- [ ] No confidential market positioning
- [ ] No leadership planning details
- [ ] Nothing you wouldn't want a customer to see

**Personal appropriateness**:
- [ ] Would you be comfortable if your manager saw this?
- [ ] Would you be comfortable if a customer saw this?
- [ ] Does this represent your views appropriately?

## Secondary Application Planning

When creating content, consider upfront:

**Primary purpose**: What is this content for originally?
**Secondary potential**: Where else could this be valuable?
**Sanitization needed**: What would need to change?
**Additional work**: How much adaptation required?

**Example planning**:
```
Primary: P2 post on Foundation ring management philosophy
Secondary potential:
- LinkedIn post (needs generalization, remove VIP-specific terms)
- Blog post (needs expansion, industry framing)
- Slack summary (needs compression to key insight)

Sanitization: Remove lifecycle ring terminology, generalize
"Foundation accounts" to "smaller accounts in your portfolio"

Additional work: LinkedIn = 30 min, Blog = 2 hours
```

## Quick Reference

**For Slack summary of anything**:
1. What's the one headline?
2. Why should they care? (one sentence)
3. What happens next? (if applicable)

**For LinkedIn from internal content**:
1. Extract one transferable insight
2. Remove all identifying details
3. Add personal perspective
4. End with question

**For blog from P2**:
1. Expand context for external audience
2. Add industry framing
3. Include more personality/opinion
4. Remove confidential specifics
5. Add external examples if helpful
