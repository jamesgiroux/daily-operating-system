Your brain shouldn't have a landlord.

Four ideas behind DailyOS that challenge how productivity tools work.

The pattern

Every productivity system ends the same way.

You set it up with the best intentions. It works for two weeks. Then friction wins. You skip the translation step. The system gets out of sync. Now catching up is more work than starting over. So you start over. New tool, same cycle.

Good intentions
Set up system (folders, tags, rules, dashboards)
Friction to use it (translate this into that format)
Take path of least resistance (skip the translation)
"I know it's recorded somewhere"
Never go back and look
System gets out of sync
More work to catch up than to abandon it
Guilt about wasted effort
New tool, new hope, good intentions... [repeat]

The road to productivity hell is paved with good intentions. Every tool in this cycle was designed to be maintained by you. When you stopped maintaining, it stopped working. DailyOS was designed to break the loop — not by demanding more discipline, but by removing the maintenance tax entirely.

The guilt isn't laziness. It's a design problem.

The idea

An executive assistant for everyone.

What does an executive assistant actually do? They're the filter. The layer between you and all the stuff. They reduce cognitive burden so you can focus on work that matters. They handle the gathering, the scheduling, the preparation — so you can handle the thinking and deciding.

That service transforms how leaders work. So why is it reserved for people senior enough to justify a $60–150K salary on their behalf?

Human EA

$60–150K/yr

Reserved for executives. Requires headcount. Scales with hiring. Knows your context through months of working together.

AI-native EA

$20/mo

Available to everyone. Runs on your machine. Scales with every transcript and email. Knows your context because it reads everything.

The account manager deserves an EA. The project lead deserves an EA. The individual contributor juggling twelve stakeholders and five customer calls a day deserves someone handling the cognitive overhead so they can focus on what they're actually good at.

AI changes the equation. DailyOS is the result.

The architecture

Your local machine is the only integration layer that works.

Nobody talks about this: enterprise tools don't consolidate. They isolate. Think about where your information lives right now.

  • Only accessible in Salesforce
  • Only accessible in Google Docs
  • Only accessible in Gong
  • Only accessible in Slack
  • Only accessible in your company's tool of choice

Each tool is building a moat around your data. The "value" they sell is visibility to the group, but they extract a tax: your information becomes hostage to their database layer. On any given day, you're hopping between ten apps. Not because it's efficient. Because your data is scattered across a dozen kingdoms that won't talk to each other.

Every enterprise platform promised to be the place where everything comes together. None delivered. They can't. Their business model depends on keeping you inside their walls.

Your local machine is the one place that has access to everything.

Not through connectors or API sync delays. Actually there. In files you control. AI pulls from everywhere, synthesizes locally, and delivers outputs you can use. The databases stay where they are. You stop being a human router between systems.

The boundary

Productivity and collaboration are different things.

Tools have conflated these. They've built "collaboration platforms" and called them "productivity tools." But they serve different moments in your day.

Collaboration

  • Group visibility
  • Shared access
  • Real-time presence
  • Knowing what everyone is doing
  • Belongs in Slack, Docs, meetings

Productivity

  • Individual execution
  • Deep preparation
  • Synthesis and context-building
  • Getting things done heads-down
  • Belongs before and after the shared space

The productive work happens alone. Then you bring it to the group. DailyOS is for the alone part. Preparation, synthesis, context. You bring the output to your team's tools — a document ready for Google Docs, a summary ready for Slack, prep ready for the meeting.

DailyOS prepares. Your team's tools collaborate.
