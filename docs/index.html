Every productivity tool gets harder to use over time.

DailyOS gets smarter.

Every system you've tried demanded more from you the longer you had it. More tags to maintain. More folders to organize. More backlog to process. The tool that was supposed to save you time became a second job.

DailyOS inverts this. Every meeting, every transcript, every email makes it more useful. The system that's been running for six months is profoundly more valuable than the one you installed yesterday. Not because you maintained anything. Because it was paying attention.

The problem

You've been here before.

Todoist, Notion, Asana. Pick your poison. You set it up perfectly. Folders, tags, dashboards. For two weeks, it was beautiful.

Then you missed a day. Then a week. The system fell behind. And now the tool that was supposed to help you has become one more thing you're failing at.

Meanwhile, every meeting still starts with 15 minutes of context-hunting across Slack, email, docs, and your CRM. You're not doing knowledge work. You're being a human router between ten apps.

The guilt isn't laziness. It's a design problem.

Every one of those tools was designed to be maintained by you. When you stopped maintaining, they stopped working. DailyOS was designed around a different question: what happens when the user disappears for a week? The answer is always the same. The system catches up. You don't have to.

How it works

Three steps. Then it runs itself.

1

Connect your calendar and email

Link Google Calendar and Gmail. DailyOS reads your schedule and correspondence to build context. Read-only. It never touches your data.

2

Add your accounts and projects

Tell DailyOS what you work on. Drop in meeting transcripts and documents. It starts building intelligence immediately.

3

Open your morning

Every day at 6 AM, the system runs. By the time you have coffee, your meetings are prepped, your actions are prioritized, and your emails are triaged.

Built for

People who manage relationships, not just tasks.

  • Account managers walking into five customer calls a day
  • Customer success leads tracking renewals across a portfolio
  • Project managers synthesizing context from a dozen stakeholders
  • Anyone whose job is knowing what's happening across multiple people and commitments

What DailyOS is not

  • Not a note-taking app
  • Not a task manager
  • Not a calendar replacement
  • Not a collaboration tool

DailyOS is for the alone part of work. Preparation, synthesis, context. You bring the output to your team's tools.

Your data

Everything lives on your machine.

Files you can open, read, move, or delete. Not rows in our database. Not behind our login. Sync however you want. Walk away anytime with everything you've built.

Local-first

Your data lives on your machine. No cloud dependency for core functionality.

Open format

Standard files any tool can read. The app is one view. Claude, ChatGPT, or whatever comes next can use the same workspace.

Open source

GPL-3.0. Read the code, fork it, contribute. No black boxes.

No lock-in

Don't like DailyOS? Take your files and go. We earn your usage, we don't trap it.
