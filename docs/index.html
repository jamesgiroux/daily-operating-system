The problem

You're the human router
between 10 apps.

Every meeting starts with 15 minutes of context-hunting. Slack threads, email chains, shared docs, CRM notes. By the time you piece it together, the meeting is half over.

You bought the productivity tool. Set it up perfectly. Folders, tags, dashboards. For two weeks, it was beautiful. Then life happened. You skipped a day. Then a week. The system fell behind.

The guilt isn't laziness. It's the system's fault.

The reframe

What if it wasn't you anymore?

What if the system connected to your calendar and email, read your meeting transcripts and documents, and built a living picture of your work?

What if it learned your accounts, your projects, your people -- then used all of it to prepare every single day?

The system operates. You leverage.

How it works

Three steps. Zero maintenance.

1

Connect

Link your Google Calendar and Gmail. DailyOS reads your schedule and email. It never sends, modifies, or deletes anything.

2

Learn

Add your accounts and projects. Drop in transcripts and documents. DailyOS builds intelligence about your work automatically.

3

Open

Every morning, your day is ready. Meetings prepped, actions surfaced, emails triaged. You walk in prepared.

What you see

Your morning dashboard.

Everything you need for the day. Your schedule with prep status. Your actions. Your priority emails. One screen, no hunting.

The retention story

It compounds.

Every meeting, every transcript, every email makes the system smarter. Your accounts build up intelligence over time. Your people develop relationship histories. Six months in, DailyOS knows your work better than you do.

Week 1

  • Calendar is prepped
  • Emails are triaged
  • Actions are tracked

Month 2

  • Full account intelligence
  • Stakeholder relationship maps
  • Proactive risk alerts
  • Meeting history across people

Your data

Your files. Your machine.
Your intelligence.

Everything DailyOS knows lives as markdown and JSON files on your machine. No cloud database. No data lock-in. The app is one interface -- Claude Desktop, ChatGPT, or any AI tool can read the same files.

Your brain shouldn't have a landlord.
