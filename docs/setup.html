Get Started with DailyOS

From download to first briefing in under 10 minutes.

Prerequisites

macOS (Apple Silicon)

DailyOS runs natively on Mac. Intel and other platforms coming soon.

Claude Code

DailyOS uses Claude Code for AI enrichment. Requires a Claude Pro ($20/mo) or Max subscription.

Get Claude Code →

Google Workspace

Calendar and Gmail integration. Personal Google accounts work too.

1

Download

Download the latest release from GitHub.

Download DailyOS v0.7.0

macOS Gatekeeper: macOS will block the app because it's not signed by Apple. To allow it:

xattr -cr /Applications/DailyOS.app

Or: System Settings → Privacy & Security → Open Anyway

2

Connect Google

When you first open DailyOS, it will ask to connect your Google account.

  1. Click Connect Google in Settings
  2. Sign in with your Google account
  3. Allow calendar and email access
  4. You'll see a confirmation in the app

DailyOS reads your calendar and email to prepare your day. It never modifies, sends, or deletes anything without your explicit action.

3

Set Up Your Workspace

Choose a folder on your machine. DailyOS creates the structure.

~/Documents/DailyOS/
├── _today/          ← Your daily briefing
├── _inbox/          ← Drop files for processing
├── _archive/        ← Past briefings
├── Accounts/        ← Your customers/clients
└── Projects/        ← Your projects

Everything lives in this folder. Markdown and JSON files you own.

4

Add Your Accounts & Projects

The onboarding wizard helps you add the accounts and projects you work on. DailyOS uses these to build intelligence and prep your meetings.

You can always add more later from the app.

5

Your First Morning

Your first briefing runs at 6:00 AM. Open DailyOS tomorrow morning.

You'll see your schedule prepped, emails triaged, and actions surfaced.

"Open the app. Your day is ready."
