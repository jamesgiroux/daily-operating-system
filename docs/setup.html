Before you start

Three things you need.

macOS (Apple Silicon)

Runs natively on M-series Macs. Intel and other platforms coming.

Claude Code

DailyOS uses Claude Code for AI enrichment. Requires Claude Pro or Max ($20/mo+).

Get Claude Code →

Google Workspace

Calendar and Gmail integration. Personal Google accounts work too.

1

Download DailyOS

Grab the latest release from GitHub.

Download v0.7.0

macOS Gatekeeper: The app isn't signed by Apple yet. You'll need to allow it:

xattr -cr /Applications/DailyOS.app

Or: System Settings → Privacy & Security → Open Anyway

2

Connect Google

First launch asks to connect your Google account.

  1. Click Connect Google in Settings
  2. Sign in with your Google account
  3. Allow calendar and email access
  4. Confirmation appears in the app

DailyOS reads your calendar and email to prepare your day. It never modifies, sends, or deletes anything without your explicit action.

3

Choose your workspace

Pick a folder. DailyOS creates the structure.

~/Documents/DailyOS/
├── _today/       # Your daily briefing
├── _inbox/       # Drop files for processing
├── _archive/     # Past briefings
├── Accounts/     # Your customers and clients
└── Projects/     # Your projects

Everything lives in this folder. Markdown and JSON files you own. Open them in any editor, sync them with any tool, read them with any AI.

4

Add your accounts and projects

The onboarding wizard walks you through it. Add the accounts you manage, the projects you're tracking. DailyOS uses these to build intelligence and prep your meetings.

You can always add more later.

5

Open your first morning

Your first briefing runs at 6:00 AM. Open DailyOS tomorrow morning and your day is laid out. Schedule prepped. Emails triaged. Actions surfaced.

Day 1 is thin. The system has one day of context. By week 2, you'll feel it working. By month 2, you'll wonder how you operated without it. That's not a promise. That's how compounding intelligence works.

"Open the app. Your day is ready."

It starts modest. It doesn't stay that way.
