Meeting mouth

This is where all signals arrive.

The briefing is not a stack of sections. It is a flow model: the meeting is the point where history, people, unresolved work, and future intent converge into one room and one conversation.

How to use it

The room becomes a downstream event.

This concept helps the user understand not just what exists, but how different strands of signal are arriving at the meeting moment. It is good for accounts where momentum and unresolved work matter as much as stakeholder dynamics.

Talking points at the river mouth

What should surface in the conversation

  • Celebrate Phase 1 completion as the dominant upstream proof.
  • Propose the Phase 2 timeline with confidence, not apology.
  • Name Alex's transition and define the handoff path.
  • Close legal and NPS follow-up so they stop muddying the channel.
Why it is different

It emphasizes causality.

Most briefings freeze the world into categories. This one treats intelligence as motion. The user sees what is flowing into the room, what is likely to collide there, and what needs to be redirected before the meeting starts.
