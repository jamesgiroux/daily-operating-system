# DailyOS v1.4.0 Architectural Strategy

**Status:** Draft (unsigned). Synthesis of the April 2026 ADR cycle. Becomes live strategy on founder endorsement. Until then, read as "where the author thinks the architecture is going," not "what DailyOS has committed to."
**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Scope:** ADRs 0100–0119 (April 2026 cycle) plus /red-team hardening pass
**Audience:** Founder, eng lead, anyone picking this up cold

## A note on velocity

This document is written for AI-native execution. DailyOS ships at velocity measured in days, not sprints. v1.2.1 shipped in roughly four days. Twenty ADRs were drafted in two. Implementation cycles are hours-to-days per discrete piece of work, not weeks.

That changes what the real constraints are. The scarce resources for AI-native software are:

- **Taste and judgment** — choosing the right abstractions when build time is nearly free.
- **Measurement** — knowing whether a bet is paying off when you can't run 20-sprint A/B tests.
- **Commitment** — holding architectural boundaries under commercial pressure when you can implement almost anything in a day.
- **Verification** — confirming the output is actually correct when speed can outpace correctness.

The scarce resources are **not**: engineering capacity, refactor cost, cycle time, or the ability to rebuild.

Throughout this document, timelines are given in days-to-short-weeks unless an external dependency (Glean SLA, model release cadence, enterprise sales cycle) genuinely takes longer. When the doc says "v1.4.0," read "whatever ships next" — usually the same or next week.

## Executive summary

Between April 18 and April 20, 2026, DailyOS accumulated twenty new architectural decisions (ADRs 0100–0119). Written in isolation and at speed, they collectively amount to a **substrate rebuild**: the system moves from a feature-per-command intelligence pipeline to a typed capability runtime with first-class claims, mandatory provenance, computable trust, mode-aware execution, durable invalidation, and structural evaluation.

The shift has a name now. [ADR-0118](../decisions/0118-dailyos-as-ai-harness-principles-and-residual-gaps.md) calls DailyOS an **AI harness** — the runtime infrastructure around an LLM that manages context, verification, memory, and feedback. The harness thesis (from Anthropic's engineering posts, Nov 2025 and Mar 2026) is that **harness quality dominates incremental model capability** for any task longer than a single context window. DailyOS is betting on this thesis in writing.

This document is three things: **what the twenty ADRs decide in aggregate** (not one by one), **a critique of the strategy** after red-team pressure, and **a path forward** that names the bets, the risks, the off-ramps, and the metrics that tell us if the investment is paying off.

TL;DR for impatient readers:

- Six substrate themes mapped by dependency, not tidy layers. The dependency graph is load-bearing; the layer diagram was narrative.
- At AI-native velocity, v1.4.0 lands in days-to-short-weeks, not 6 weeks. The real risks are judgment risks, not capacity risks.
- The "harness bet" is real and recent. It needs instrumentation — metrics that tell us we're winning, not vibes. Baselines must be captured before targets are meaningful.
- Four bets, four named off-ramps. Each bet has a signal that abandons it and a cost of abandonment.
- Explicit non-goals included so "what we're not doing" is actually named.

## Situation

DailyOS is a native macOS chief-of-staff app for Customer Success. The product promise: open the app, your day is already assembled with more context than any competitor. The moat is depth-before-interaction — briefings, meeting prep, risk detection, proactive commitments — all composed from meetings, emails, transcripts, CRM data, and accumulated signals.

Before April 2026, the intelligence layer was a collection of feature-local pipelines. Each command assembled its own context, each pipeline wrote its own mutations, each output shipped with implicit trust. When a new signal source arrived — Gong sentiment, Linear ticket, Clay enrichment — every feature that could benefit had to be touched manually. Quality was a narrative, not a measurement.

The trajectory was known internally: local optimizations, good vertical depth, but the harness around the LLM was ad-hoc. Users experienced this as occasional ghost behavior — AI re-populating fields they had removed, confident assertions that couldn't be traced, silent drift between runs. Engineering experienced it as rewrite-by-rewrite velocity: every new cycle required touching the same places.

## Complication

Three pressures arrived at once in Q1 2026.

**Pressure 1 — the Intelligence Loop ceiling.** Adding a signal source cost a PR per consuming feature; quality was unmeasurable at the capability level; reversibility bugs (DOS-12, stakeholder roles) revealed that user corrections were routinely overwritten by enrichment. The architecture had ossified even as the product was still growing.

**Pressure 2 — the horizon.** Enterprise buyers have started showing up in conversations. Multi-agent scenarios (agent-authored claims, cross-analyst review) are visible on the 12-month horizon. These futures demand architectural seams set now — control planes, propose/commit boundaries, publish guards — because retrofitting them later costs an order of magnitude more.

**Pressure 3 — vocabulary.** Anthropic published two harness-engineering posts (Nov 2025, Mar 2026) naming a pattern DailyOS had been building informally. The vocabulary made the design explicit. It also made the bet explicit: harness quality now dominates model capability for long-horizon tasks. Either DailyOS invests in its harness or its ceiling is the model's baseline.

Concurrent work streams (trust compiler from v1.8.0, temporal primitives, abilities runtime, signal granularity audit) converged into a single v1.4.0 Abilities Runtime project. Twenty ADRs landed in one cycle. They were written correctly but in isolation — no macro synthesis, no aggregate critique.

This document is that synthesis.

The ADRs cluster thematically into six concerns, which is how the rest of this document discusses them. The thematic groupings below are for *human reading*; they are not an execution plan. Execution plan follows the dependency graph above.

### Claims, sources, temporal fabric — the substrate

**Claims become first-class addressable records.** [ADR-0113](../decisions/0113-human-and-agent-analysis-as-first-class-claim-sources.md) + [ADR-0107](../decisions/0107-source-taxonomy-alignment.md) move intelligence out of 27 JSON columns on `entity_assessment` and into an `intelligence_claims` table with per-claim identity, actor (user / agent / human / system / external), source taxonomy (`DataSource` enum with Glean as aggregator preserving downstream lineage), and append-only history via supersede pointers. Tombstones consolidate three existing dismissal mechanisms. The role-type ghost-resurrection bug becomes structurally impossible.

**Change over time becomes stored state, not on-demand computation.** [ADR-0109](../decisions/0109-temporal-primitives-in-the-entity-graph.md) introduces five trajectory primitives (HealthCurve, EngagementCurve, RoleProgression, RiskTrajectory, RelationshipStrength) maintained by dedicated refresh abilities. Every ability that asks "what changed since last meeting" reads from one canonical view instead of reconstructing per-invocation.

**Signals get typed and durable.** [ADR-0115](../decisions/0115-signal-granularity-audit.md) introduces a `SignalType` enum (today: string across 318 call sites), a compile-time-enforced policy registry, granular coalescing keys per signal type, and durable invalidation jobs with cycle detection via chain ancestry. Replaces the current depth-limited-drop design that would silently leave outputs stale.

**Mutations become disciplined.** [ADR-0101](../decisions/0101-service-boundary-enforcement.md) + [ADR-0103](../decisions/0103-maintenance-ability-safety-constraints.md) + [ADR-0104](../decisions/0104-execution-mode-and-mode-aware-services.md) force every domain mutation through `services/` with a typed `ServiceContext` carrying `ExecutionMode` (Live / Simulate / Evaluate). `check_mutation_allowed()` guards every write. Maintenance abilities (unattended, scheduled, blast-radius-capable) operate under 11 structural safety constraints including idempotency, budgets, snapshot-diff audit, dry-run conformance.

### Abilities, provenance, scoring — the contract

**Every capability is an ability.** [ADR-0102](../decisions/0102-abilities-as-runtime-contract.md) defines the runtime contract: capabilities are named, typed, versioned functions in one of four categories (Read / Transform / Publish / Maintenance) with call-graph-enforced effect classification. All surfaces — Tauri app, MCP, workers, evaluation harness — invoke exclusively through the ability registry per [ADR-0111](../decisions/0111-surface-independent-ability-invocation.md). [ADR-0112](../decisions/0112-migration-strategy-parallel-run-and-cutover.md) stages the rollout: substrate, then pilot migration, then parallel-run with ≤1% divergence gate, then cutover.

**Glean is the primary intelligence engine.** [ADR-0100](../decisions/0100-glean-first-intelligence-architecture.md) made the bet that Glean MCP's cross-source reach (Salesforce, Zendesk, Gong, Slack) and 3–6× latency advantage beat local PTY enrichment. Abilities orchestrate around Glean-first; PTY is fallback. Consequence: Glean becomes a hard dependency for the best UX; two-tier quality emerges (Glean users vs not).

**Every output is provenanced, fingerprinted, and scored.** [ADR-0105](../decisions/0105-provenance-as-first-class-output.md) makes `Provenance` envelope mandatory on every `AbilityOutput<T>` — identity, temporal context, source attribution, composition tree, field-level attribution, trust assessment. [ADR-0106](../decisions/0106-prompt-fingerprinting-and-provider-interface.md) extends the provider interface to return metadata enabling canonical prompt hashing — replay, regression classification, and prompt-vs-input fault isolation all become possible. [ADR-0114](../decisions/0114-scoring-unification.md) consolidates scoring primitives scattered across `signals/bus.rs`, `signals/decay.rs`, `signals/fusion.rs`, and `intelligence/health_scoring.rs` into a single pure-function `scoring::factors` module. Trust Compiler (DOS-5) and health scoring consume the same primitives.

**Surfaces render provenance differently.** [ADR-0108](../decisions/0108-provenance-rendering-and-privacy.md) defines per-surface rules: Tauri shows three-level expansion; MCP shows actor-filtered summary with internal IDs redacted; P2 publications show source classes only. LLM-generated explanation text is sanitized before any render.

### Evaluation — the gates

**Quality is measurable at test time.** [ADR-0110](../decisions/0110-evaluation-harness-for-abilities.md) defines hermetic fixtures (state.sql + inputs.json + provider_replay.json + expected_output.json + expected_provenance.json) scored by exact match (Read), LLM-as-judge (Transform), or snapshot-diff (Maintenance). Fingerprint-based regression classification distinguishes ProviderDrift / PromptChange / InputChange / CanonicalizationBug / LogicChange. CI-enforced regression gate before any ability becomes authoritative.

**Quality is measurable at runtime.** [ADR-0119](../decisions/0119-runtime-evaluator-pass-for-transform-abilities.md) adds a lightweight evaluator LLM call after any opt-in Transform ability. Rubric reuses [ADR-0110](../decisions/0110-evaluation-harness-for-abilities.md)'s `quality.toml` — one source of truth, no drift between test-time and runtime. Low composite score triggers a single retry with critique attached. Sampling + cheap judge tier + failure-suspicion focus keeps cost tractable.

**Harness components are auditable for staleness.** [ADR-0110](../decisions/0110-evaluation-harness-for-abilities.md) §9 amendment adds stripped-harness fixtures: quarterly eval with named components disabled (dimension_split, consistency_repair, runtime_evaluator, etc.) to test whether each scaffold still earns its keep as models improve. The harness itself gets the harness treatment.

**Principles are named.** [ADR-0118](../decisions/0118-dailyos-as-ai-harness-principles-and-residual-gaps.md) maps ten harness principles onto the v1.4.0 substrate, establishing shared vocabulary. Nine of ten are implemented by in-flight substrate; five residual gaps identified and all closed by specific ADRs or tracked issues.

### Boundaries — the frontier

**Control plane boundary set before a control plane exists.** [ADR-0116](../decisions/0116-tenant-control-plane-boundary.md) declares the rule that no future server component sees user content — metadata only, content never. The `DbKeyProvider` trait seam lands now at near-zero cost, replacing direct `get_or_create_db_key` calls with a trait that can later accept `TenantKmsWrapped` for enterprise BYOK without a schema change. Cryptographic offboarding (revocation by breaking key lease, not wiping device) becomes possible.

**Publish boundary set before the first publish ability exists.** [ADR-0117](../decisions/0117-publish-boundary-pencil-and-pen.md) defines the Pencil/Pen protocol: every publish is a two-phase operation with a reviewable draft, user-confirmed commit, outbox-backed delivery with idempotency keys, and honest retraction semantics per destination. Types land in v1.4.0; infrastructure in v1.4.1; first real publish ability in v1.4.2. `AgentWithAuthority` shape exists but is hard-rejected at every surface until v1.5.0+.

Before vs. after, one row per meaningful shift:

| Dimension | Before (Q1 2026) | After (v1.4.0 substrate) |

## Tradeoffs

At human-paced velocity, the tradeoffs in this space are about capacity — substrate depth vs. shipping velocity, type enforcement vs. experimental agility. At AI-native velocity, those tradeoffs mostly dissolve: both can be high. What remains is a different shape of tradeoff.

### 1. Substrate correctness vs. substrate shipped

Building a typed, pure, deterministic scoring factor library is a day's work. Building it *correctly* — with the right abstractions, the right boundaries, inputs that generalize to future composers — is a judgment problem that can't be accelerated by velocity. The tradeoff is **correctness vs. shipped**.

The failure mode is not "we couldn't ship it." It's "we shipped it wrong, and the wrong shape becomes load-bearing before we notice." Mitigation: the end-to-end slice in Path forward exercises the abstractions before they're applied to more abilities. If the slice requires awkward wrapping, the abstraction is wrong.

### 2. Typed contract vs. contract legibility

Typed abilities with mandatory provenance, fixtures, category enforcement, and the SPEC-TEMPLATE readiness rubric is a much higher bar than "write a Tauri command." At AI velocity, the *cost* of clearing the bar isn't hours — it's comprehension. A Claude Code session picking up work must hold the ability contract + provenance envelope + execution mode + service boundary + eval harness conventions in its context. A human contributor the same.

Watch: when velocity-limiting friction shows up, does work route *around* the contract ("just write a quick command for this one thing") or *through* it ("yes, this is an ability, let me register it")? If around, the contract is dead. The SPEC-TEMPLATE labels + Linear `spec:ready` gate make the convention visible; holding the convention is discipline, not tooling.

### 3. Control plane seam cost vs. future liability

[ADR-0116](../decisions/0116-tenant-control-plane-boundary.md) is the most unverified bet in the set. The "metadata only, content never" rule has never been tested against real product pressure ("but the enterprise buyer wants to see what their team is working on"). The trait seam is cheap now; the boundary *principle* will be expensive to defend when real commercial pressure arrives. Precedent matters: if the first real enterprise conversation softens the boundary ("just this once, metrics only"), the precedent rots the rule. The strategic question is whether DailyOS has the pricing power and principle commitment to hold it.

This is the tradeoff that AI velocity does not help with. Commercial discipline is not a build problem.

## Three horizons (at AI-native velocity)

Timelines recalibrated for AI-native execution. External dependencies (Glean, model releases, enterprise sales cycles) keep their real-world durations; internal build times compress.

### H1 — Substrate, days-to-short-weeks

**Ships:**
- `ExecutionMode` + mode-aware `ServiceContext`.
- `SignalType` enum + policy registry scaffolding (Phase 0 of [ADR-0115](../decisions/0115-signal-granularity-audit.md)).
- `intelligence_claims` table + `actor` + `claim_state` + `claim_corroborations` + `agent_trust_ledger` + `claim_contradictions` tables + service functions.
- `DbKeyProvider` trait + `LocalKeychain` implementation + `invalidate_cache()`.
- Publish protocol types (Rust only; no tables yet).
- Evaluation harness operational for 1–2 pilot abilities (`get_entity_context`, `prepare_meeting`).
- `scoring::factors` consolidation of existing primitives.
- Runtime evaluator research + enrichment spike (DOS-241) → a design doc committed, not speculation.
- One ability driven end-to-end through every substrate surface (§ Path forward, action 1).

**Does not ship (explicit non-goals, see below):**
- Agent commit policy active (types ship; gate config stays `immediate`).
- Publish outbox + workers + any real publish ability.
- Health composer migration (lives on its own timeline; not blocked on 1.4.0).
- KMS integration (v2.x).
- Old tombstone table retirement (consolidation follows DOS-7 activation).
- Analysis Inbox surface.
- Debug trace surface (DOS-250 shell possible, not required).

**Success criteria:**
- All R1 scope-cut items have explicit follow-on issues filed; none deferred to "later."
- Evaluation harness green for the two pilot abilities.
- Zero regression in user-facing briefing behavior through parallel-run validation.
- Substrate test coverage ≥80% on new code paths.
- CI time doesn't regress by more than 20%.
- **One ability demonstrates end-to-end: services → claim → provenance → trust → fixture eval → rendered surface. This is the proof.**

### H2 — Activation, short-weeks

Once H1 ships, H2 starts immediately. No cycle gap.

**Ships:**
- Enrichment refactor landing on the abilities runtime (DOS-241 executing).
- Commit policy flips to `gated` for agent claims; Analysis Inbox shell.
- Runtime evaluator pass on first Transform abilities (sampling at 0.2).
- Durable invalidation jobs + load-test gate green.
- Publish outbox + infrastructure; `publish_to_p2` as first real publish ability.
- Debug trace surface (DOS-250).
- Claim history consolidation; old tombstone tables retired.
- Trust Compiler shadow-mode → production rendering.
- Temporal primitives Phase 2 (HealthCurve, RiskTrajectory, RelationshipStrength).

**Success criteria:**
- Every Tauri command invokes through the ability registry (≥90%).
- Runtime evaluator correlates with user correction rates (κ ≥ 0.5 or equivalent).
- Zero ghost-resurrection incidents in logs.
- Glean-first path handles ≥80% of enrichment load.
- Divergence monitor green through cutover.

### H3 — Scale, external-cadence-gated

Internal build time compressed; H3 duration is set by enterprise sales cycles, cross-device sync design, and multi-agent validation — not build velocity.

**Ships:**
- Health composer migration to shared factors.
- Enterprise features (multi-user, BYOK via `TenantKmsWrapped`, control plane polling for revocation) *gated on actual enterprise demand arriving*.
- Harness-stripping first results (quarterly cadence); at least one component retired or confirmed.
- Agent trust ledger fully active; multi-agent scenarios viable.
- Cross-device sync, team intelligence (respecting the [ADR-0116](../decisions/0116-tenant-control-plane-boundary.md) boundary).

## Explicit non-goals for v1.4.0

At AI-native velocity, the question isn't "what are we deferring for capacity." It's "what are we choosing not to build because it isn't worth building now." Named here so they don't get silently re-added:

- **No new user-facing features in v1.4.0.** Zero. The substrate needs the full cycle. Users see no visible change.
- **No performance optimization.** Several ADRs introduce small performance costs (provenance envelope size, dual-write during migration, evaluator LLM calls). We measure; we don't preemptively optimize.
- **No observability platform beyond existing logs.** The eventual metrics dashboard (§ metrics below) is lightweight and sqlite-queryable. No Datadog, no Grafana, no OpenTelemetry.
- **No enterprise pilots.** [ADR-0116](../decisions/0116-tenant-control-plane-boundary.md) lays the boundary; zero conversations with real enterprise prospects happen until v1.4.0 substrate is signed off. Sales pressure during substrate work tends to reshape architecture the wrong way.
- **No new LLM providers.** The provider interface gets the [ADR-0106](../decisions/0106-prompt-fingerprinting-and-provider-interface.md) amendment; the set of usable providers does not grow.
- **No mobile, no web app, no desktop-beyond-macOS.** Tauri + MCP + the one devtools surface.
- **No multi-agent live demos.** [ADR-0113](../decisions/0113-human-and-agent-analysis-as-first-class-claim-sources.md)'s `AgentWithAuthority` shape exists. No demo of it runs outside fixture tests until v1.5.0+.

## The bets

Four strategic bets in writing. Each has a falsification signal and a named off-ramp. "Off-ramp" means: if the bet is losing, what do we do, and what does it cost to undo? At AI-native velocity, undoing is cheap if we've held the bet to a tight scope. The goal is to name the scope so we can actually abandon if required.

### Bet 1 — The harness thesis

**Claim:** Harness quality now dominates model capability for tasks past a single context window ([ADR-0118](../decisions/0118-dailyos-as-ai-harness-principles-and-residual-gaps.md), sourced from Anthropic Nov 2025 / Mar 2026).

**If right:** The substrate investment compounds. Every signal source deepens every ability. Quality is computable. New models slot in through the provider interface without architectural rework.

**If wrong:** The substrate is overhead. A leaner product — commands-and-prompts, less scaffolding, more trust in the model — wins.

**Weakness to acknowledge:** The Anthropic posts describe multi-session coding agents. DailyOS is a read-heavy synthesis app. The analogy transfers but isn't proven. A more honest framing: *"At horizons longer than a single context window, harness quality is load-bearing — even if it's less dominant than it is for coding agents."*

**Falsification signal:** Harness-stripping audits ([ADR-0110](../decisions/0110-evaluation-harness-for-abilities.md) §9) show that with dimension split, consistency repair, and the runtime evaluator all stripped, output quality (by rubric composite) drops by less than 10% on current models. If the harness is invisible to the judge, it's invisible to the user.

**Off-ramp:** Retire the scaffolding identified as low-strip-delta. Collapse the runtime evaluator into test-time only. Keep the claim substrate (already necessary for enrichment correctness). Cost: a handful of edits at AI velocity. The substrate tables and services remain because they solve the ghost-resurrection bug and enable trust, regardless of whether the harness thesis holds.

### Bet 2 — Glean as the intelligence engine

**Claim:** Glean MCP's cross-source reach + speed justifies making it the primary path, with PTY Claude Code as fallback ([ADR-0100](../decisions/0100-glean-first-intelligence-architecture.md)).

**If right:** DailyOS ships enterprise-grade context assembly without building the ingestion itself.

**If wrong:** Glean's API changes, outages, or pricing become single-point-of-failure; two-tier UX fragments the product.

**Supply chain concern** the doc was missing: Glean is a startup. Acquisition, pivot, pricing model change, or breaking API change can reshape this bet overnight. No SLA currently negotiated.

**Falsification signal:** Glean p95 latency exceeds PTY fallback for two consecutive weeks, OR Glean availability drops below 99.5% in any calendar month, OR Glean ships a breaking API change with less than 30 days notice, OR pricing changes materially.

**Off-ramp:** Flip the provider interface precedence — PTY first, Glean second. The provider abstraction from [ADR-0091](../decisions/0091-intelligence-provider-abstraction.md) + [ADR-0106](../decisions/0106-prompt-fingerprinting-and-provider-interface.md) makes this a config change, not a rewrite. Cost: latency regression for Glean-connected users until local ingestion alternatives develop.

### Bet 3 — The enterprise seam is close-to-zero-cost

**Honest claim** (replacing R0's "zero-cost seam" framing): [ADR-0116](../decisions/0116-tenant-control-plane-boundary.md)'s trait seam costs roughly a few hours of work now — trait definition, `DbKey` newtype with zeroization, two provider impls (sync + async), compatibility wrapper around existing call sites — and saves a larger refactor at enterprise-integration time. It is not free. The claim is that paying a small cost now defers a larger cost later, when commercial pressure would force it to happen under worse conditions.

**Real costs already identified** (codex flagged, R1 addressed):
- Dual sync/async trait variants (cognitive load).
- DI migration is an `open_with_provider()` second path alongside zero-arg `open()`; the seam doesn't replace the existing pattern, it adds a second one.
- Zeroization dependency (`zeroize` crate).
- Process-wide `OnceLock<String>` cache now needs `invalidate_cache()` path.

**If right:** v2.x enterprise integration is a drop-in new provider implementation, not a rewrite.

**If wrong:** The abstraction boundary is slightly off. Real KMS work requires re-doing the trait.

**Weakness to acknowledge:** The seam is never exercised against a real enterprise conversation in v1.4.0. By construction, we can't know it's right until v2.x. The "zero cost insurance" framing was misleading; this is a hedge with a small premium and a large payoff *if* the bet wins.

**Falsification signal:** First real enterprise prospect conversation — does the KMS integration story hold up without architecture revision?

**Off-ramp:** Delete the async trait and the `DbKeyProvider` altogether. Restore `get_or_create_db_key(&Path)` as the single code path. The `LocalKeychain` implementation becomes the only one. Cost: a handful of edits. The `DbKey` newtype + zeroization we keep — those were improvements regardless of seam.

**Hardening action:** Commit (via founder sign-off) to a rule: any ADR amendment that softens the "metadata only, content never" boundary of [ADR-0116](../decisions/0116-tenant-control-plane-boundary.md) requires founder approval and a named compensating control. This is a commercial commitment, not an architectural one. Without it, the boundary is a footnote.

### Bet 4 — Append-only claims + evaluator create measurable quality

**Claim:** Claim history + runtime rubric scoring + harness-stripping audits turn quality from narrative into metric.

**If right:** We observe "this cycle's prompt changes lifted actionability +0.3, lowered groundedness −0.1" and act on it.

**If wrong:** The rubric doesn't correlate with user perception; we optimize the rubric instead of the product.

**Falsification signal:** After a 3–5 day calibration run — 100 enrichment outputs scored by both the runtime evaluator and a human reviewer (you) — inter-rater reliability is poor (κ < 0.5 or the equivalent signal from ranked agreement). If the evaluator scores don't track human judgment on our data, the evaluator optimizes the wrong thing.

**Off-ramp:** Strip the runtime evaluator ([ADR-0119](../decisions/0119-runtime-evaluator-pass-for-transform-abilities.md)); keep test-time eval ([ADR-0110](../decisions/0110-evaluation-harness-for-abilities.md)). Cost: we lose the production self-correction path. We keep the regression gate. The substrate underneath (claims, provenance, trust) is valuable regardless.

**Hardening action:** Before rolling out the runtime evaluator past the first pilot ability, run the calibration. If κ < 0.5, invest in rubric design before expansion, not after.

## Critique

The ADRs were written cleanly individually but have aggregate problems.

### The dominant risk is judgment, not capacity

Earlier framings of this document (and the /red-team pass against them) leaned on execution-capacity risk — "can we ship it." At AI-native velocity, that framing is wrong. The risks that matter are:

- **Did we choose the right abstractions?** ([ADR-0116](../decisions/0116-tenant-control-plane-boundary.md)'s boundary testing only at v2.x; [ADR-0113](../decisions/0113-human-and-agent-analysis-as-first-class-claim-sources.md)'s actor taxonomy untested against second-user scenarios; [ADR-0114](../decisions/0114-scoring-unification.md)'s "pure factors" not yet exercised against production scoring.)
- **Can we tell if we're winning?** The evaluator correlation question is the sharpest form of this.
- **Will we hold the boundaries under pressure?** Commercial commitment, not architecture.

Judgment-risk mitigations don't look like "add more engineers." They look like:

- End-to-end slice before expanding scope. The substrate slice in Path forward proves abstractions on one ability before applying them to ten.
- Calibration runs before rollout. Bet 4's κ check is 3–5 days of work and prevents a quarter of rubric-chasing.
- Named off-ramps. If a bet is losing, we know what to do.

### Complexity budget

Each ADR adds surface area: at least 8 new tables across 0113, 0115, 0116, 0117, 0119; at least 6 new traits; 2 new registries; new fixture infrastructure; new config files. Individually reasonable; aggregated, the cognitive overhead for a new contributor is real.

Note: "new contributor" at AI-native velocity is often a Claude Code session picking up work, not a human being onboarded. The architectural map needs to be readable by both.

**Mitigation:** `.docs/SPEC-TEMPLATE.md` and the spec readiness rubric address part of this. The 12-surface architectural vet list per issue forces contributors to walk the surfaces. Add a top-level `ARCHITECTURE.md` (onboarding artifact distilled from this strategy doc) so "the map" is reachable in one click from the repo root.

### The "principle holds" question for ADR-0116

Every argument for [ADR-0116](../decisions/0116-tenant-control-plane-boundary.md)'s metadata-only rule assumes DailyOS has the commercial discipline to hold the rule under pressure. This has never been tested. The first enterprise conversation will ask a reasonable question ("let the admin see team activity"), and the architectural answer ("no, here's the privacy-preserving workaround") has to be more expensive than the sale is worth. That's a product-leadership commitment, not an architectural one. The ADR cannot enforce it.

**Mitigation:** Commit now (via this document) to a specific rule: any ADR amendment that softens [ADR-0116](../decisions/0116-tenant-control-plane-boundary.md)'s metadata-only rule requires founder approval and a named compensating control. The bar exists before the pressure does.

### Aggregate provenance surface area

[ADR-0105](../decisions/0105-provenance-as-first-class-output.md) makes the Provenance envelope mandatory on every ability output. Fine. But the envelope is large (identity, temporal context, sources, composition tree, field_attributions, prompt fingerprint). Across a composed ability tree, the aggregate provenance can exceed the content it describes. For Read abilities this is mostly fine (not rendered); for Transform abilities streaming to surfaces, it's a bandwidth and parsing cost.

**Mitigation:** [ADR-0108](../decisions/0108-provenance-rendering-and-privacy.md) §6 size budgets exist but are not enforced. Add a hard limit: any provenance envelope > 64 KB fails CI. Forces either richer summarization or shallower composition; either is better than silent bloat.

### The evaluator's signal-to-noise ratio is unvalidated

[ADR-0119](../decisions/0119-runtime-evaluator-pass-for-transform-abilities.md) adds an LLM call per ~20% of Transform invocations. If the evaluator's critique correlates with user perception of quality, the retry-with-critique loop earns its cost. If it correlates weakly or noisily, we double our cost for vibes-grade improvement.

**Mitigation:** Before expanding beyond the first pilot ability, run a 30-day A/B: cohort A gets evaluator + retry; cohort B doesn't. Compare user correction rates, field-override rates, and product usage. If delta is < 3% on any of those, the evaluator needs rubric work before rollout.

### Ship-gate discipline for signal granularity

[ADR-0115](../decisions/0115-signal-granularity-audit.md) §6 load-test gate is the right instinct — 1,000 `ClaimTrustChanged`/min sustained for 10 min before claim-level `PropagateAsync` ships. But "ship gate" in practice often means "we'll run it later." If the gate doesn't execute automatically (weekly CI or similar), it will ship untested.

**Mitigation:** Load test runs on a scheduled GitHub Action weekly against a synthetic dev environment. Failing that, it runs before any release that touches claim-level signals. "Later" is too soft.

### Missing: operational metrics plan

Twenty ADRs worth of architecture, not one shared dashboard. The harness bet is measurable (see Bet 1); the Glean bet is measurable (Bet 2); the quality bet is measurable (Bet 4). But there is no single artifact that says "here are the 10 metrics we watch to know the strategy is working." This is a gap.

**Mitigation:** File a follow-on issue to assemble a `HARNESS-METRICS.md` or similar with the specific telemetry each ADR assumes. Without it, each ADR's success criteria exist in isolation and the aggregate strategy has no report card.

## Metrics to watch

Selected for: they tell us if the bets are working, not if the individual ADRs shipped.

### Baselines before targets

A metrics table is theater without baselines. "Trend up" isn't a target; "trend up from X to Y" is. Before the dashboard ships:

1. Instrument the metrics listed below in production (or dev with production-shaped data).
2. Measure baselines for 1–2 weeks.
3. Only then set targets in terms of absolute numbers, not directions.

At AI-native velocity, instrumentation is a single-session job per metric. Baseline collection is calendar-bound, not work-bound.

### The metrics

| Metric | What it tells us | How to read it |
|---|---|---|
| Runtime evaluator composite score per ability per week | Bet 4: is quality improving or drifting? | Set baseline; watch variance and trend against baseline |
| User correction rate per ability per week | Bet 4 + 1: does computed quality correlate with perceived quality? | Baseline then target reduction; correlate week-over-week with composite |
| Ghost-resurrection incident count per week | Substrate effectiveness ([ADR-0113](../decisions/0113-human-and-agent-analysis-as-first-class-claim-sources.md) tombstones) | Baseline from current logs (likely non-zero); target zero within 2 cycles after tombstone consolidation |
| Glean path p95 latency | Bet 2: is Glean still the right primary? | Baseline now; watch for sustained regression above PTY fallback |
| Glean path availability | Bet 2 single-point-of-failure risk | Baseline over 30 days; red-line at 99.5% |
| Harness-strip-delta per audit per component | Bet 1: does each scaffold still earn its keep? | Baseline established at first audit; retirement candidates flagged at ≤ 0.02 across three audits |
| Abilities runtime adoption — % of capabilities invoked through registry | Contract discipline | Baseline after H1 substrate lands (expected very low); target ≥ 90% by end of H2 |
| Substrate test coverage on new code | Substrate quality | Target ≥ 80% on new code paths from H1 on |
| CI time delta per cycle | Complexity budget watch | Baseline now; red-line at +20% cycle-over-cycle |
| Time from signal source added to first downstream ability benefit | The "depth compounds" claim | Baseline from most recent source addition; target decreasing over time |

All of these are queryable from local SQLite + logs. No observability vendor. One `scripts/strategy_dashboard.sql` per metric, `scripts/strategy_dashboard.md` rendering them quarterly.

## Conclusion

Twenty ADRs in two days is unusual only at human pace; at AI-native pace, it's a morning's work. The unusual part is that they cohere. The ADRs reference each other, the R1 revisions harden each other, and the substrate — claims, provenance, abilities, evaluation — is legible as a single design, not a pile of decisions.

What's in front of the work now is the harder half: delivering on an architecture that is honest about being mostly type-shape rather than running code. The spec template, readiness rubric, and this strategy document together give the discipline to execute.

The first meaningful measure of whether this worked comes with v1.4.0. Not "did the types compile" — that's a given. The measure is: **did one ability go end-to-end through every substrate surface that exists, and did the runtime evaluator correlate with your own judgment on its output?** If yes, the substrate is proven and activation continues. If no, we have shipped intentions.

At AI-native velocity, shipping intentions fast is still shipping intentions. Velocity doesn't substitute for judgment.
