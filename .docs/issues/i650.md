# I650 — Outlook chapter editorial redesign

**Area:** Frontend / Account Detail / Design
**Priority:** P1
**Version:** v1.1.0 (Phase 6)
**Effort:** Medium
**Source:** User feedback (2026-03-29). The Outlook chapter crams renewal confidence, expansion signals, and contract context into a confined area. The LLM hedges in expansion text, creating confusing output. The component doesn't breathe like the rest of the editorial dossier.

---

## Problem

The AccountOutlook component tries to be three sections in one:

1. **Renewal confidence** — a structured label (high/moderate/low) with risk factors
2. **Expansion signals** — opportunities with ARR impact, stage, and strength
3. **Contract context** — contract type, dates, ARR

These are distinct information domains forced into a single flow. The result:
- The headline mixes structured confidence with free-form narrative
- Expansion potential lacks its own clear heading — readers don't know what the italic text is about
- Risk factors render as a bulleted list that breaks the editorial rhythm
- Contract context is a data dump at the bottom
- The whole section feels like a dashboard widget, not a magazine chapter

## Design Direction

Each domain should be its own section with editorial rhythm:

**Renewal** — headline confidence statement + risk factors as prose, not bullets. "The renewal is tracking well, with two watchpoints: the CTO's vendor review and Q2 budget constraints."

**Growth** — clear "Growth Opportunities" heading. Each signal as its own editorial block with stage indicator, not a list. "EMEA expansion could add $80K ARR — currently exploring with 3 teams."

**Commercial Reality** — contract facts rendered like the vitals strip or provenance labels. Type, dates, ARR as structured data with source attribution. Not prose.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Outlook chapter has three visually distinct sub-sections with their own headings
2. Renewal section: confidence as editorial headline, risk factors as flowing prose (not bullet list)
3. Growth section: "Growth Opportunities" heading (not "Expansion Signals"), each opportunity as a standalone block
4. Commercial section: structured data layout matching the editorial pattern
5. Each sub-section has generous whitespace between it and the next
6. LLM expansion narrative has a clear "Growth" context label — no guessing what the text refers to
7. Follows DESIGN-SYSTEM.md tokens, magazine aesthetic, section rules over cards
