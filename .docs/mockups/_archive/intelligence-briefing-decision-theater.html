Opening scene

The meeting is a stage. The briefing should rehearse the scene.

This concept reframes intelligence as live performance. Who is on stage? What conflict is likely to surface? Which lines need to land? What should not become the scene's accidental center? It turns the briefing into a run-of-show for judgment, not just a dossier of facts.

Scene stakes

Goal: move from celebrating Phase 1 into confident Phase 2 scoping. Risk: the room gets dragged into transition anxiety, budget hesitation, or unresolved legal details. Hidden challenge: let the detractor signal inform the meeting without letting it seize the script.

Script

The sequence the briefing wants the room to follow

Beat 1

Open with proof, not housekeeping

Line to land: Phase 1 completed ahead of schedule, support volume is down, and the account now has a credible base for Phase 2. This establishes the meeting's opening energy.

Beat 2

Pivot quickly into the next arc

Line to land: propose April start and June delivery for advanced analytics, with APAC expansion as the optional second act.

Beat 3

Name the fragility without letting it dominate

Line to land: Alex's transition is understood and a handoff plan is being treated as part of Phase 2 readiness, not as a destabilizing surprise.

Beat 4

Resolve the loose backstage work

Line to land: legal SOW and NPS detractor follow-up are underway, with owners and dates, so the room does not have to improvise around them.
