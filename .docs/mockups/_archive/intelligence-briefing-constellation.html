How to read this

The briefing becomes a force map.

Instead of organizing by chapter or list type, the page shows what is pulling the meeting toward confidence, hesitation, or distraction. The user can see, before reading a word of detail, which signals are central and which are peripheral.

Why this concept is interesting

It visualizes influence, not just information.

A typical briefing tells you what is present. This tells you what matters because of its pull on the conversation. It is useful for relationship-heavy meetings where the center of gravity is more important than the chronology.
