# Intelligence Briefing Concepts

This set reimagines the briefing itself, not just the page styling.

Common payload across all concepts:

- `Acme Corp Weekly Sync` prep fixture
- linked Acme account intelligence
- meeting context, attendees, since-last changes, strategic programs
- talking points, risks, open items, executive read, and references

Files:

- `intelligence-briefing-constellation.html`
- `intelligence-briefing-signal-river.html`
- `intelligence-briefing-decision-theater.html`
- `intelligence-briefing-terrain.html`

## 1. Constellation

**Idea:** a briefing as a live relationship sky.

The meeting sits at the center. Stakeholders, risks, programs, and actions orbit it with different gravitational weights. Useful when the most important question is not "what happened?" but "what is pulling this meeting off course?"

## 2. Signal River

**Idea:** a briefing as a flow of signals converging into the room.

History, open work, risks, and agenda items stream toward the meeting moment. Useful when the value of the briefing is showing causality and momentum rather than static categorization.

## 3. Decision Theater

**Idea:** a briefing as a staged moment with actors, tensions, and beats.

The page behaves like a pre-show run sheet. It highlights who is on stage, what scene is likely to happen, what lines need to land, and where the conflict may surface.

## 4. Terrain

**Idea:** a briefing as a navigable decision landscape.

The account is rendered like terrain: high ground, fault lines, ridges of momentum, valleys of uncertainty. Useful when the user needs to feel the shape of the situation rather than consume another chapter list.

## Shared Goal

These are intentionally not editorial magazine pages.

They ask a different question:

> If DailyOS invented its own native form for intelligence briefing, what might that form be?
