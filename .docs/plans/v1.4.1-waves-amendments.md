## Amendment 1 — Stage-3b closure is contingent on the v1.4.2 WordPress spike outcome

`v1.4.1-waves.md` §437-442 defines six data-sufficiency criteria for stage-3b
closure (volume, surface coverage, band distribution, alert canary,
threshold-delta ADR, no regression). The proof-bundle audit at
`.docs/plans/v1.4.1-waves/W3-stage-3b-proof-bundle.md` (2026-05-15) records
that PR #260 satisfies 2 of 6 (criterion 1 volume + criterion 6 no regression,
the latter vacuously). Criteria 2 (5 surfaces), 3 (3 bands ≥50), 4 (canary),
and 5 (threshold-delta ADR) are unmet.

Four of the five surfaces named in criterion 2 (meeting detail, entity detail,
actions, email) are macOS-app surface call sites. v1.4.2 is intentionally a
WordPress-surface spike. If that spike succeeds, WordPress becomes the
primary surface and the four macOS call sites become obsolete work. Filing
Linear tickets to wire them now would be churn against a likely-deprecated
surface layer.

Criteria 3, 4, and 5 are downstream of criterion 2: tuning a trust-band
threshold requires diverse surface inputs to feed the trust compiler; the
threshold-delta ADR documents the tuning result; the canary verifies the
alert path on a tuned threshold. The full chain is surface-contingent.

**Amendment:**

1. Stage-3b is recategorized as **instrumentation-complete,
   data-sufficiency-pending** rather than failed-or-open. PR #260's
   feature flag, schema, and briefing call site stand as the
   instrumentation milestone.

2. W6's hard precondition at `v1.4.1-waves.md` line 655 ("stage-3b closure")
   is **relaxed to stage-3b instrumentation-complete** for the purpose of
   unblocking W6. W6 starts against the partial baseline.

3. Final stage-3b closure is **deferred** to a v1.4.2-outcome decision:

   - **If the v1.4.2 WordPress spike succeeds** and WordPress becomes the
     primary surface, the four macOS-surface call-site requirements
     (criterion 2 partial) are **superseded**, not satisfied. New tickets
     for WordPress surface wiring + tuning + ADR + canary are filed under
     v1.4.2 or a successor wave plan, not retroactively under v1.4.1.

   - **If the v1.4.2 WordPress spike fails or is rolled back**, the original
     residual tickets enumerated at the bottom of the proof bundle (12 items
     covering surface wiring, tuning, ADR, canary, L3/L4/L5, retro) are
     filed at that decision point and W3 stage-3b returns to OPEN until they
     are completed.

4. The proof bundle stays in git as the audit trail. It is not amended to
   claim closure; it remains an accurate record of what shipped and what did
   not at the v1.4.1 wave-completion mark.

5. The v1.4.1 release notes (when written) must state that trust-band
   rendering operates on live trust scoring, not shadow trust scoring; the
   shadow pipeline is instrumented but not tuned, and the band distribution
   it produces today is heavily skewed and should not be relied on.

This amendment is **not** a retroactive scope reduction of v1.4.1: stage-3b
closure was always intended to gate W6, and W6 still ships in v1.4.1. The
amendment narrows what "stage-3b closure" means for the W6 unblock decision
specifically, and routes the residual to wherever the surface layer ends up
living after the WordPress spike resolves.

- `feedback_wire_existing_substrate_not_future_producer` — acknowledged: the

  wire it. This amendment does not pretend otherwise; it routes the wiring
  decision to v1.4.2 spike outcome.
- `feedback_no_deferrals_period` — not violated: scope agreement is being
  revised based on new strategic information (the v1.4.2 spike framing),

## Amendment 2 — L2 is wave-scoped, not per-agent-PR

`v1.4.1-waves.md` §659 (and equivalent lines in other wave merge gates)
reads "L2 diff approvals on N PRs" where N is the number of agents in the
wave. This phrasing assumes each agent's work ships as an independent PR
with its own L2 panel. In practice, multi-agent waves are tightly coupled:
agents share files (release-gate mandatory-bundle declarations, registry
exports, shared types), meta agents consume sibling outputs, and the wave
itself is the cohesive feature being shipped — not the individual streams.

Per-agent L2 cycles in this shape produce churn: each PR's L2 reviewer
re-discovers the same class-pattern findings (release-gate wiring,
acceptance-criteria coupling, integration with siblings), agents block on
each other's PRs, and the wave-level completeness gap between issue-level
ACs and wave-level scope is never reviewed.

**Amendment:**

1. L2 is run **once per wave**, against the integrated diff of all agents'
   work, not N times against N per-agent PRs.

2. The L2 panel reviews the wave bundle: codex adversarial + code-reviewer +
   the wave's designated domain reviewer (qa-expert for W6, security-auditor
   for trust-boundary waves, etc.). Unanimous APPROVE required, bounded by
   the union of (a) per-issue acceptance criteria for each agent's ticket
   AND (b) wave-level completeness — does the integrated diff satisfy the
   wave's stated outcome.

3. After unanimous APPROVE, the wave ships as a single PR (or as N
   chained PRs that merge atomically). PR title and body reference all
   agents' tickets.

4. This supersedes the literal "N PRs" reading of any wave merge gate
   line. Where the wave plan says "L2 diff approvals on N PRs," interpret
   as "L2 diff approval on the wave's integrated diff." The N PRs language
   was a clumsy artifact of early wave-protocol drafting.

5. Acceptance criteria are combined at review time — the L2 panel reads
   each agent's ticket AC AND the wave's overall scope, and rejects if
   either layer has gaps. Wave-level completeness is a first-class
   reviewer concern, not a downstream L3 problem.

This amendment applies to all waves in v1.4.1 (and is the recommended read
for future waves until contradicted). James's 2026-05-15 message is the
authority.
