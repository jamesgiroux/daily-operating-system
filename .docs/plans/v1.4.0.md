# v1.4.0 — Publication + Portfolio + Intelligence Quality

**Thesis:** v1.0.0 makes DailyOS the operating system for an individual account manager (including Glean-first intelligence in Phase 5). v1.1.0 adds lifecycle intelligence and briefing depth. v1.2.0 closes action loops. v1.3.0 rebuilds the report engine. v1.4.0 makes it the operating system for leadership. Publication gives ICs a way to share curated narrative with VPs. The portfolio page gives VPs an aggregate surface. The surfacing model teaches the app when to interrupt vs when to stay quiet.

## ADR-0100 Impact on v1.1.0 (recorded 2026-03-14)

The Glean-first intelligence architecture (ADR-0100, v1.0.0 Phase 5) fundamentally changes the portfolio thesis. Key implications:

**Anyone can populate any account.** With Glean connected, a VP can add "Globex Holdings" and get full intelligence in 30 seconds — health score, risks, stakeholders, competitive context, support history — without ever having had a meeting with that account. The distinction between "my accounts" (personal context) and "org accounts" (need IC to publish) collapses. Every account is an org account you choose to watch.

**Publication shifts from data access to curated narrative.** The original design had ICs publish reports so VPs could see account data. With Glean-first, the VP already has the data. Publication becomes about sharing the IC's human judgment — their editorial assessment, leadership asks, relationship context that only comes from being in the room. The IC's value isn't data access, it's interpretation.

**What this changes for each issue:**

| Issue | Original Role | Post-ADR-0100 Role |
|---|---|---|
| **I533** (publication) | VPs need published reports to see account intelligence | VPs have their own Glean-sourced intelligence. Publication is for curated IC narrative — leadership asks, relationship assessments, strategic recommendations that require human judgment. |
| **I534** (portfolio reader) | Reads published reports from Drive as the VP's only intelligence source | Supplementary — augments the VP's own Glean-enriched accounts with IC editorial perspective. Less critical than before. |
| **I492** (portfolio page) | Consumes published reports for aggregate view | Consumes the VP's own Glean-enriched accounts directly. Published IC narratives are additive context, not the primary data source. The VP's book of business is self-populated via Glean discovery. |
| **I532** (surfacing thresholds) | Teaches app when to surface intelligence changes | More important, not less. A VP watching 50 Glean-enriched accounts needs aggressive filtering. Without surfacing thresholds, the briefing becomes a wall of noise. |

**What this changes for v1.2.0:**

The "team intelligence" scope (conflict resolution, cross-IC aggregation) simplifies dramatically. There's less to aggregate when everyone can see the same Glean-sourced intelligence. The differentiation between ICs becomes their personal context (meetings, transcripts, corrections), not their access to account data. Cross-IC conflict resolution may only matter for user corrections (IC-A edited a risk that IC-B disagrees with) — not for base intelligence, which comes from the same Glean source.

## Principles

1. **Publication is curated narrative, not data access.** With Glean-first (ADR-0100), every user can populate any account with org intelligence. ICs publish to share their human judgment — leadership asks, relationship assessments, strategic recommendations — not raw data.
2. **Glean is the org brain, DailyOS is the personal brain.** Glean retrieves facts from across the org. DailyOS interprets them through the lens of the user's priorities, relationships, and corrections. Neither replaces the other.
3. **Proactive, not reactive.** The system doesn't wait for the user to ask. It detects gaps, searches for answers, and surfaces what matters — on the user's schedule, not in real-time.
4. **Learn from behavior.** The surfacing model, the feedback loop, and the Bayesian source weights all improve with use. Ship reasonable defaults, then learn.
5. **Graceful degradation.** Every Glean-dependent feature works without Glean — just with less data. No feature breaks when Glean is disconnected.

## Phase 1: Publication + Portfolio Surface

**Goal:** Intelligence flows from ICs to VPs via Google Drive. The portfolio page gives VPs a single surface for their entire book.

| ID | Title | Notes |
|----|-------|-------|
| **I533** | Publication engine | Google Drive output layer. Reports published as PDF/Google Doc to Shared Drive. Auto-publish per report type. JSON sidecar for structured metadata. Glean auto-indexes within ~10 min. New OAuth scope: `drive.file`. Spec: `.docs/issues/i533.md`. |
| **I534** | Portfolio reader | Reads published reports from Shared Drive. Parses JSON sidecars for structured data (health, bucket, ARR, risks). Feeds into portfolio page and Portfolio Health report. "via {IC name}" attribution. 1-hour cache. Spec: `.docs/issues/i534.md`. |
| **I492** | Portfolio Health page | Editorial aggregate surface. Bucket view (default): Growth Focus / At Risk Saveable / At Risk Save Unlikely / Autopilot. Heatmap toggle. Exception list. Renewal timeline. Both local and published accounts. Spec: `.docs/issues/i492.md`. |

**Acceptance criteria:**

1. IC generates VP Account Review for Acme. Clicks "Publish." PDF appears in `{Shared Drive}/DailyOS Intelligence/Acme Corp/` within 10 seconds. JSON sidecar alongside it.
2. VP's DailyOS reads the Shared Drive. Portfolio page shows Acme Corp with health score, bucket, and trend from the sidecar — alongside VP's own locally-managed accounts.
3. Published accounts show "via {IC name}" attribution. Click opens Drive URL.
4. Auto-publish: IC enables auto-publish for VP Account Review. Generates report. Published automatically.
5. Portfolio page bucket view: every account (local + published) classified into exactly one bucket with rationale.
6. Portfolio page works with 5 local accounts + 15 published accounts — layout scales.
7. Report > 7 days old shows "Stale" badge on portfolio page. > 30 days shows "Outdated."
8. Portfolio Health Summary report (I491) incorporates published accounts — clearly labels "my accounts" vs "team accounts."
9. Glean discoverability: published report searchable in Glean within 15 minutes (manual verification).
10. `drive.file` scope only — DailyOS cannot access files it didn't create.

## Phase 2: Intelligence Quality

**Goal:** The surfacing model learns when to interrupt vs when to stay quiet — the chief of staff knows what matters.

| ID | Title | Notes |
|----|-------|-------|
| **I532** | Intelligence surfacing threshold model | Significance scoring (5 factors: magnitude, importance, temporal relevance, pattern detection, novelty). Four tiers: critical (briefing + portfolio), notable (meeting-proximate), context (meeting prep only), background (silent). Daily budget (max 5 in briefing). Per-account cooldown. Decay. Feedback-driven learning via I529/I530. Spec: `.docs/issues/i532.md`. |

**Acceptance criteria:**

1. Health score dropping 20+ points in 7 days surfaces in daily briefing Attention section.
2. Champion departure surfaces as critical (>= 0.8) in briefing and portfolio exceptions.
3. Routine enrichment update (< 5 point change) does NOT surface — stays as background.
4. Daily budget: 10 accounts all changing → briefing shows top 5 + "and 5 more."
5. Decay: item unacknowledged 5 days drops from Critical to Notable. 10 days → Context.
6. Feedback loop: thumbs-down on surfaced item reduces future significance for that signal type.

## End-to-End Verification (before tagging)

1. **Publication flow.** IC generates VP Account Review for Acme. Auto-publishes to Shared Drive. PDF + JSON sidecar appear. VP's DailyOS shows Acme on portfolio page within minutes.
2. **Portfolio synthesis.** VP has 3 local accounts + 12 published accounts from ICs. Portfolio page shows all 15. Bucket classification covers all. Exception list surfaces declining published accounts.
3. **Surfacing.** Account health drops 25 points overnight. Open DailyOS next morning. Briefing Attention section: "Acme Corp: health declining — champion went quiet, no meeting in 21 days." Thumbs-down it. Next occurrence of similar signal surfaces with lower significance.
4. **Graceful degradation.** Disconnect Glean. All features work — just with less data. No errors, no blank states.

7. `pnpm tsc --noEmit` — clean.

## Explicitly Out of Scope

- **Server-side sync / shared database** — ADR-0099 withdrawn. Publication replaces sync.
- **Glean Indexing API push** (structured metadata) — v1.2.0+ if needed. Google Drive publication is sufficient for v1.1.0.
- **Slack bot / Slack notifications** — evaluate Glean's Slack bot first. DailyOS Slack integration is post-v1.1.0.
- **Team-level aggregation beyond publication** — cross-IC correction conflicts, shared editorial voice. v1.2.0+ (simplified by ADR-0100 — base intelligence is shared via Glean, only corrections diverge).
- **Local AI providers** (I432-I434) — v2.1.0.
- **Theming** (I483) — post-1.0, tracked separately.
- **Custom report templates** — v1.1.0 ships designed templates.
