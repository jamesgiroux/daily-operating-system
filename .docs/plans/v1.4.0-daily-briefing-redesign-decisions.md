## D1 — `/week` route redundancy

**Question.** Does the Weekly Forecast page still exist after Daily Briefing redesign ships?

**What Daily Briefing redesign already covers from /week:**
- DayStrip (yesterday | today | tomorrow nav with preview text) → covers "what's the next/previous day"
- DayChart in the schedule section → covers "shape of today"
- Click-through navigation between adjacent days

**What /week currently does that Daily Briefing redesign does not:**
- 5-day week shape at a glance (parallel readiness indicators across days)
- "Earlier" / "Today" / "Ahead" timeline grouping with relative day labels
- Single surface to compare meeting load Mon→Fri before committing prep time

**Options:**
- **D1.a — Deprecate /week entirely.** DayStrip arrow click-through is the only multi-day surface. Lose the parallel-week glance.
- **D1.b — DayStrip "expand" state.** Click a label or use a keyboard shortcut to expand DayStrip into the current 5-day week shape, inline above the briefing. Drops the standalone /week route.
- **D1.c — Keep /week as a different lens.** Reframe Weekly Forecast as "preparation queue" — shows briefing readiness across days, not the day-by-day shape. Different job from Daily Briefing redesign.
- **D1.d — Promote /week.html to the canonical multi-day view, redirect.** Treat Daily Briefing redesign as today-only and keep /week as the longer-horizon surface.

**Recommendation:** D1.b. Keeps the affordance accessible but doesn't carry a separate route's worth of code/spec/component baggage. /week deprecates, WeekPage.tsx + module CSS + reference HTML archive.

**Impact on tickets:**
- Adds: 1 ticket for DayStrip expand state (Track B), 1 ticket for /week deprecation cleanup (Track E).
- Removes: nothing (week wasn't in the original list).
- Surface inventory: -1 routed surface, DayStrip primitive grows in scope.

## D2 — Actions & emails on the briefing

**Question.** Do orphan actions and "worth your attention" emails still appear on the daily briefing, or do they leave entirely?

**Current briefing has:**
- Lifecycle changes section (account state transitions)
- Overdue actions (with check-to-complete)
- Today's actions (orphan, not tied to a meeting)
- "Worth your attention" emails with priority dot + entity chip + score reason
- Aging-toward-archive notice
- View-all links → /actions, /emails

**Daily Briefing redesign has:**
- Watch section (parked items + InferredActionSelector)
- Moving section (overnight changes to accounts/people)
- No standalone email list, no orphan-action list, no lifecycle-changes group, no aging notice

**Options:**
- **D2.a — Push everything to /actions and /emails.** Briefing is meeting-centric and signal-light. Daily email triage moves entirely off the briefing. Cost: loses the "scan first, decide later" daily ritual.
- **D2.b — Add a dedicated section back.** New "Today's Attention" or "Inbox" section between Schedule and Moving. Restores email triage on the briefing but partially reverts the redesign simplification.
- **D2.c — Fold into Moving + Watch with explicit kinds.** Moving rows accept `kind="email"` and `kind="action"`. Watch rows accept "expiring" or "aging" items. Briefing keeps its shape; emails/actions land in existing slots.
- **D2.d — Hybrid: meetings own their actions, watch owns the rest.** Per-meeting prep actions render in MeetingSpineItem footer (already does). Orphan actions + emails land in Watch. Lifecycle changes land in Moving.

**Recommendation:** D2.d. Aligns with the redesign principle that the briefing is for orientation, not work-execution. Meetings carry their own prep work (already in design). Orphan actions and emails become Watch rows when they need attention; otherwise they live on /actions and /emails. Lifecycle deltas fit naturally in Moving.

**Impact on tickets:**
- MovingRow pattern needs `kind="account-state-change"` variant.
- WatchRow pattern needs `kind="orphan-action"` and `kind="email"` variants.
- Adds: 1 ticket for /actions and /emails surface uplift (now they're the primary home for those flows).
- Adds: 1 ticket for "what triggers a Watch row vs goes to /actions" service rule.
- Removes: nothing — but redefines the size of Watch + Moving services.

## D4 — Abilities runtime UI integration

**Question.** Where does v1.4.0 W6 abilities runtime UI surface in the new briefing?

**v1.4.0 W6 ships these display-impacting outputs:**
- **DOS-320 — per-claim trust band.** Every claim that renders in a UI surface needs a trust-band visual (likely on the existing `IntelligenceQualityBadge` and a new per-field band).
- **DOS-411 — claim-backed lifecycle UX.** When the user corrects something, the correction sticks. UI needs: (a) inline correction affordance, (b) "this was corrected" visual, (c) revert path.
- **DOS-412 — sensitivity-aware rendering.** Claim outputs the user shouldn't see (or shouldn't see in this context) get redacted/elided. UI needs to communicate "something here is hidden."
- **DOS-218/219 — pilot ability outputs.** Recommendations and predictions ABOUT the user's day. Currently no design home.

**The redesign reference shows none of these.** So it's either incomplete or implicitly assumes these surface in non-briefing contexts.

**Options:**
- **D4.a — Reference is incomplete; add ability UI to redesign.** Wire trust bands into MeetingSpineItem subtitle. Wire claim-lifecycle "corrected" indicator into Moving. Wire ability recommendations into Lead or a new "Recommended" section.
- **D4.b — Daily Briefing redesign is a v1 reference; ability UI ships as v2.** Land redesign as drawn, then layer ability UI on top in a follow-up wave. Acceptable only if the data models are forward-compatible.
- **D4.c — Ability UI lands on entity surfaces, not the briefing.** Trust bands appear on /account, /meeting, /person — not on the briefing roll-up. Briefing stays opinion-light.
- **D4.d — Ability UI defines a new briefing section.** "Predictions" or "Drift" — predictions for the day/week. Lives between Schedule and Moving. New pattern.

**Recommendation:** D4.a + D4.d combination.
- **D4.a:** Trust bands on MeetingSpineItem (already a fresh/developing badge slot — it just needs to use the trust-band shape). Claim-lifecycle "corrected" indicator on Moving rows. Sensitivity elision is a global rendering rule, not a section.
- **D4.d:** Add a "Predictions" / "Forecasts" section that consumes pilot-ability output (DOS-218/219). Sits between Schedule and Moving as the "what the system thinks may happen" beat. New pattern.

**Impact on tickets:**
- 2 new design tickets: trust-band integration on MeetingSpineItem, claim-lifecycle visual on MovingRow.
- 1 new pattern ticket: `PredictionsSection` (or whatever it's called).
- 1 new service ticket: ability output → briefing-view-model contract.
- v1.4.0 W6 timing: this means redesign ships *with* W6 outputs wired in, not after. Compresses schedule.

# Locked decisions

## D1 — DEPRECATE /week

`/week` route, `WeekPage.tsx`, `WeekPage.module.css`, and `reference/surfaces/week.html` archive. The user does not use weekly forecast (calendar app covers that job). However the underlying calendar service still computes ±7 days so DayStrip preview text (yesterday short summary, tomorrow short summary) and click-through navigation work end-to-end. Same data shape; narrower display.

## D2 — Moving = signal feed; Watch = action triage

**Moving** is no longer "account state changes." It is the **signal feed**: 2–3 entities where significant change has occurred in the last 24 hours, sourced from email + Gong + Zendesk + Slack + Linear (via Glean) + meetings + lifecycle changes. Restraint rules:
- 2–3 entities maximum visible at rest.
- Lede: 1–2 sentences max per entity.
- Each row clicks through to the entity detail page.

**Watch** absorbs the action lane: suggested actions (with InferredActionSelector), open actions, parked items. The UI is more adaptive there to support those affordances.

**Lifecycle changes land in Moving** as one signal kind among many.

### D2.1 — Moving row shape (locked: Shape A)

Three-column grid matching the existing `.dspine-moving-row`, enriched:
- Left column: entity name (mono caps) + state pill ("Renewing ↑")
- Center column: 1-2 sentence lede + signal feed (3–5 typedot items)
- Right column: stacked provenance stats (Health, Stage, Confidence, Last touch — varies by entity kind)

Signal feed items are: typedot · when · what · (optional) thread-mark button. Typedot kinds: meeting, action, email, lifecycle, gong-call, zendesk-ticket, slack-thread, linear-issue. New `SignalDot` primitive owns the kind→color/glyph mapping.

### D2.2 — /emails surface

`/emails` survives as the raw inbox view (the "I want to scan all my email" surface). Moving consumes the same email signals but curates 2-3 entities; users who want the unfiltered view go to /emails.

### D2.3 — /actions surface

`/actions` survives as the canonical orphan-action list and per-meeting action drilldown. Watch surfaces the actions that need attention TODAY; /actions is the full backlog.

## D3 — Aggressive cull

Anything in the current briefing with no redesign home archives. Concretely:
- `BriefingMeetingCard` and its expansion panel (`theRoom`, `prepGrid`, `meetingActions`) → archive. Expansion content's destiny: MeetingDetailPage already owns this; verify and complete.
- `editorial-briefing.module.css` rules for `lifecycleGroup`, `priorityItem` family, `replyMeta`, `agingNotice`, `prepFlag`, `priorityCheck`, `capturedSummary` → archive.
- `EmailEntityChip` → archive (EntityChip primitive replaces).
- `MeetingCard` (the routing-list variant) → keep for /week deprecation period, then evaluate if any consumer remains.

No "extract abstraction first" — if it doesn't have a home in the new layout, it's dead.

## D4 — a + d combination

**D4.a:** Trust bands wire into MeetingSpineItem (replace the existing fresh/developing badge with the trust-band shape) and Moving SignalDot (claim-lifecycle "corrected" indicator on the relevant signal). Sensitivity-aware rendering is a global rule, not a section.

**D4.d:** New **Predictions** section between Schedule and Moving. Default render is a single-line minimal state (e.g. "3 predictions today · expand"). Click expands inline to show the predictions list (DOS-218/219 ability outputs). Restraint: predictions don't push other content down at rest.
