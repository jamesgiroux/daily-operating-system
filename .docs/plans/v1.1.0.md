# v1.1.0 — Lifecycle Intelligence + Briefing Depth

**Thesis:** v1.0.0 made DailyOS an intelligence surface. v1.1.0 makes the intelligence *act*. The system detects lifecycle transitions automatically from email signals, Glean CRM changes, and calendar patterns — then tells you what it did in the daily briefing. You confirm with one click or correct what's wrong. No management UI. No pipeline. No workbench. "It should just know."

The briefing gains depth: expansion panels reconnect prep previews that regressed during the v1.0 redesign, and lifecycle intelligence joins the Attention section alongside actions and emails. The account detail page stays a dossier — it shows current state with provenance, not stages to manage.

Phase 5 extends that same philosophy to account truth itself: the dossier should not just look trustworthy, it should be backed by richer DB-native facts from Glean, exact source references, explicit fact-vs-inference boundaries, and time-boxed relevance so "what matters now" stays current.

**Status:** Planning. Defined 2026-03-21 via CEO plan review + product philosophy challenge. Requires v1.0.1 to ship first.

**Origin:** A CSM had email signals confirming a Bring a Trailer renewal (signed order form) but no way to tell DailyOS "this renewal is done." The first instinct was to build lifecycle management UI — a pipeline, a breadcrumb bar, a product registry with CRUD. The philosophy challenge revealed this was CRM thinking. The DailyOS answer: the system should have detected the renewal automatically and reported it in the briefing. "It should just know."

---

## Principles

1. **"It should just know."** The system detects lifecycle transitions from signals and acts automatically. It doesn't propose and wait. It acts and reports. The user confirms or corrects.
2. **AI produces; users curate.** The briefing tells you what changed. The dossier shows current state. You correct what's wrong. You don't manage a pipeline.
3. **Zero guilt.** Nothing accumulates. No pending suggestions. No unacknowledged transitions. The system handled it. The briefing shows what happened. You review when you open the app.
4. **The dossier stays a dossier.** The account detail page is for reading and correcting, not for managing lifecycle stages, product inventories, or renewal pipelines (ADR-0084).
5. **Products are intelligence, not inventory.** Glean discovers products. AI infers them from transcripts and emails. No add/edit/remove UI. Wrong? Correct inline like any other intelligence field.
6. **Provenance is trust.** Editorial attribution ("via Salesforce," "you noted") helps CSMs know where data came from without cluttering the magazine layout.
7. **Facts and inference are different species.** ARR from Salesforce is not the same kind of truth as "procurement looks difficult." Canonical account facts must be DB-backed, source-aware, and distinct from AI synthesis.
8. **Current beats merely interesting.** The dossier and meeting briefing should foreground current and relevant intelligence. Older issues move into the record unless they are still active.

## Phase 1: Lifecycle Intelligence Engine

**Goal:** The system detects lifecycle transitions from signals and applies them automatically. The daily briefing reports what changed. The user confirms or corrects.

| ID | Title | Notes |
|----|-------|-------|
| **I623** | Lifecycle intelligence engine | Automatic signal-driven transitions. Email signals (order form signed, churn language), Glean CRM changes (opportunity stage), calendar patterns (no meetings in 30d), and time triggers (120 days to renewal) drive lifecycle state changes. Transitions reported in briefing Attention section. User clicks [Looks good] or [Fix something]. Corrections feed Bayesian weights. |

**Acceptance criteria:**

1. Account "Bring a Trailer" has email signals indicating renewal confirmed (order form signed). The system automatically advances lifecycle from "renewing" to "active," updates renewal_date to next year, and rescores health with "active" dimension weights. No user action required.
2. Daily briefing Attention section shows: "Bring a Trailer renewed — order form signed (detected from email). Health: 72 → 78. Lifecycle: Active. [Looks good] [Fix something]." Max 2-3 lifecycle updates shown.
3. User clicks [Looks good] — the change is confirmed, positive feedback signal emitted. User clicks [Fix something] — inline correction UI opens (same pattern as intelligence field corrections). Correction emits `user_correction` signal (confidence 1.0), Bayesian weights penalize the source that got it wrong.
4. Account at 120 days to contract_end: system automatically transitions lifecycle to "renewing" and reports in briefing. Account with no meetings in 30 days + declining email engagement: system surfaces "at-risk signal detected" in briefing (but does NOT auto-transition to at_risk without stronger evidence — at_risk requires compound signals).
5. Account detail page shows lifecycle as a label with provenance in the hero section: "Active · renewed Mar 15 · via email signal." No breadcrumb bar, no pipeline stages, no management UI.
6. `renewal_stage` column on accounts table tracks sub-state during renewal period (approaching, negotiating, contract_sent, signed, processed). Updated automatically by signals. Displayed as secondary text on dossier. NULL when lifecycle ≠ "renewing."
7. All automatic transitions emit `lifecycle_transition` signal via `emit_signal_and_propagate()`. Success plan milestones checked on each transition (I628).

## Phase 2: Product Intelligence

**Goal:** Products appear on the account dossier as AI-discovered intelligence context. No CRUD UI.

| ID | Title | Notes |
|----|-------|-------|
| **I624** | Product intelligence | New `account_products` table. Glean MCP query discovers products from Salesforce during enrichment. AI inference detects product mentions in transcripts and emails. Products displayed as read-only intelligence on the dossier with provenance. Wrong? Correct inline — same as any intelligence field. Corrections emit `user_correction` signal. |

**Acceptance criteria:**

1. New `account_products` table: id (INTEGER PK), account_id (TEXT NOT NULL), name (TEXT NOT NULL), category (TEXT), status (TEXT — active/churned/trial), arr_portion (REAL), source (TEXT — glean/ai_inference/user_correction), notes (TEXT), created_at (TEXT), updated_at (TEXT).
2. During enrichment with Glean connected, products queried from Salesforce via MCP chat. Products upserted with source "glean."
3. AI inference: enrichment prompts and transcript processing detect product mentions. Products upserted with source "ai_inference" and lower confidence.
4. Account dossier shows "Products & Entitlements" section: product name, status badge, source attribution ("via Salesforce," "via Gong"). No add button. No edit button. No remove button.
5. If a product is wrong, CSM clicks to correct inline (same `EditableText` pattern as other intelligence fields). Correction changes source to "user_correction" and emits signal.
6. Product data included in `build_intelligence_context()` and `gather_account_context()` — enrichment and meeting prep prompts know what products the account has.
7. Product changes emit `product_data_updated` signal.
8. Graceful degradation: without Glean, product section is empty (not hidden). No error. If AI infers products from transcripts, those appear even without Glean.

## Phase 5: Source-Aware Account Truth

**Goal:** Make account detail and meeting detail the best possible operating surfaces by promoting high-confidence Glean-backed facts into the DB, separating facts from inference, time-boxing relevance, and making every surfaced item traceable to exact source evidence.

| ID | Title | Notes |
|----|-------|-------|
| **I644** | Canonical account truth + source references | Promote factual Glean-backed account state into DB-backed account truth. Add exact source reference records for surfaced fields and split renewal timeline stage from commercial opportunity stage. |
| **I645** | Timeliness, staleness, and feedback-aware learning | Add freshness windows, current vs historical state, and a feedback model so thumbs up/down, dismiss, accept/reject, and live edits materially change the next refresh. |
| **I646** | Source-attributed account detail + meeting briefing refresh | Refresh dossier and meeting detail chapters around the richer DB-backed truth: commercial reality, relationship map, support/ops health, current themes, historical record, and source verification. |
| **I647** | Source-aware prompt propagation + downstream PTY context refresh | Update prompt shapes and context assembly so all later PTY work, meeting prep, reports, and briefing synthesis inherit the improved DB-backed account truth. |
| **I648** | Source coverage matrix + field promotion rules | Create the implementation-facing field taxonomy so every Glean-accessible field family is classified by authority, freshness, promotion destination, and target surfaces before it is shipped. |
| **I649** | Technical footprint, adoption, and service-delivery intelligence | Add structured technical footprint, entitlements, usage telemetry, and services evidence to the DB-backed assessment and surface them across `State of Play`, `Outlook`, `The Record`, and meeting briefing. |

**Acceptance criteria:**

*DB-first read model (from JSON audit 2026-03-30, absorbed into I644):*

1. **No detail page reads `intelligence.json` under any condition** — including edits and dismissals. The filesystem fallback is removed; missing DB row is an error.
2. **No detail page reads `dashboard.json`** — account/project narrative fields (overview, programs, notes) promoted to DB columns. `get_account_detail` and `get_project_detail` are fully DB-read-only.
3. **Stale comments cleaned up** — `services/accounts.rs`, `services/projects.rs`, `commands/core.rs` comments match actual implementation.

*Canonical account truth + source references (original I644):*

4. High-confidence factual fields from Glean-backed sources can promote or refresh DB-backed account facts such as ARR, renewal date, renewal likelihood, support tier, active subscriptions, growth potential, and ICP fit.
5. Every surfaced account-detail or meeting-detail fact has source metadata strong enough for verification: source system, source kind (`fact`, `signal`, `inference`), observed timestamp, and exact originating record reference.
6. `renewal_stage` no longer has to represent both timeline proximity and CRM opportunity state. Commercial stage is stored separately and rendered separately.
7. Empty or non-overridden hero vitals can be hydrated from factual source-backed DB values. User-edited vitals are never silently overwritten.
8. Current and relevant intelligence is prioritized by default. Resolved or stale issues move into historical sections such as `The Record` unless they recur or remain unresolved.
9. Thumbs up, thumbs down, dismiss, accept/reject, and live-edit corrections all change future refresh behavior by adjusting source weights, suppressing dismissed items, and injecting user overrides into later prompts. `Thumbs down` also removes the item from the current UI immediately.
10. DailyOS maintains an explicit field promotion matrix so the breadth of Glean-accessible data is mapped deliberately into canonical facts, structured assessment, historical record, meeting-only context, and report-only content.
11. Technical footprint, entitlements, adoption telemetry, and services evidence are available as structured account truth and can improve `State of Play`, `Outlook`, `The Record`, meeting briefing, and downstream PTY context.
12. `build_intelligence_context()`, meeting prep context assembly, and report assembly consume the richer DB-backed account truth so later PTY calls become more accurate without re-deriving the same facts from raw sources.

## Independent Track: AI Efficiency & Background Guardrails

**Goal:** Preserve manual AI capacity by making background PTY work slower, cheaper, and budget-aware.

| ID | Title | Notes |
|----|-------|-------|
| **I642** | Token usage observability + efficiency foundations | Metering, diagnostics, transcript dedup, and usage visibility. Establishes the measurement layer and coarse efficiency controls. |
| **I643** | Background PTY burn reduction + AI control consolidation | Fixes poller cadence, adds a dedicated background model tier, cheap automatic enrichment, rolling timeout/token pause guards, and visible `AI & Background Work` controls in System settings. |

**Acceptance criteria:**

1. Calendar and email pollers never run more frequently than configured minute intervals. Activity may defer a due poll by 60 seconds, but never accelerates cadence.
2. Google pollers stay dormant unless Google is enabled, auth is valid, and a workspace exists.
3. Automatic `CalendarChange`, `ContentChange`, and `ProactiveHygiene` work routes through the background tier and a cheap single-pass enrichment path. Manual refresh and workflow-driven work retain the richer path.
4. `CalendarChange` is debounced for 10 minutes per entity and the intelligence queue processes one entity per wake.
5. Legacy hidden model overrides are reset once to the recommended 4-tier bundle (`sonnet`, `sonnet`, `haiku`, `haiku`) and the reset is persisted.
6. Background PTY work pauses for 30 minutes when rolling 4-hour background usage exceeds 50,000 estimated tokens, when timeout rate across the last 20 background calls is >= 25%, or after 3 consecutive background timeouts.
7. Settings → System shows a visible `AI & Background Work` section with model routing, poll intervals, pre-meeting refresh window, current background AI state, and `Reset to Recommended`.
8. Settings → Diagnostics shows structured per-operation PTY attribution, per-model counts, rolling 4-hour totals, timeout rate, and background pause reason.

## End-to-End Verification (before tagging)

1. **Lifecycle flow.** Account "Bring a Trailer" is in "renewing" lifecycle. Email signals indicate renewal confirmed. System automatically transitions to "active," rescores health, updates renewal_date. Daily briefing shows: "Bring a Trailer renewed — health improved. [Looks good] [Fix something]." User clicks [Looks good].
2. **Product flow.** Account "Acme Corp" enriched with Glean connected. Products section shows 3 products from Salesforce with provenance. No add button visible. CSM notices wrong product, clicks to correct — correction emitted as user_correction signal.
3. **Provenance flow.** CSM set ARR to $137K. Glean returns $142K from Salesforce. ARR shows "$137,000 · you updated · Mar 10" with secondary line: "Salesforce shows $142,000 [Accept] [Dismiss]."
4. **Briefing depth.** Meeting expansion panel shows calendar description, The Room (attendees grouped), PrepGrid (discuss/watch/wins), "Before this meeting" checklist (max 3 actions), "Read full briefing →" link.
5. **Auto-completion.** Lifecycle transition to "active" after renewal detection checks Renewal Preparation success plan. "Contract signed" milestone auto-completes.
6. **Degradation.** Disconnect Glean. Lifecycle engine still detects transitions from email signals and calendar patterns. Product section empty (not hidden). Provenance shows "AI synthesis" and "you noted" only.
7. **Background guardrails.** Set calendar and email poll intervals in System settings, relaunch, and confirm pollers stay dormant when Google is disabled, never exceed configured cadence, and show a visible background AI pause state in Diagnostics after synthetic budget exhaustion.
8. **Model routing reset.** Start from a legacy 3-tier config, launch once, and confirm the app persists the recommended 4-tier routing bundle and exposes it in `AI & Background Work`.
9. **Canonical fact promotion.** A Glean-enriched account with Salesforce-backed ARR, renewal likelihood, support tier, and opportunity stage promotes those values into DB-backed account truth and returns exact source references in account detail and meeting detail payloads.
10. **Vitals reinforcement.** An account with empty ARR / renewal / support vitals hydrates from factual source-backed DB values. A user-edited ARR remains unchanged and instead shows a source-backed conflicting suggestion.
11. **Timeliness.** A resolved support incident older than the active window no longer appears in the active watch list or meeting brief by default, but remains visible in `The Record`.
12. **Feedback loop.** Thumbs down on a surfaced risk removes it immediately and suppresses it from the next refresh unless newer or stronger evidence exists. A live edit is injected into prompt context on the next refresh.
13. **Field breadth.** The implementation-facing field promotion matrix classifies commercial, stakeholder, support, technical footprint, adoption, services, compliance, and historical pricing fields by authority and target surface before they are shipped.
14. **Technical authority.** A Glean-enriched account can surface current app/environment footprint, entitlements, support tier/SLA, and recent services work in DB-backed dossier sections with exact source references.
15. **Product classification.** When Glean enriches Blackstone account, the Financial dimension `chat` call extracts product_classification from Salesforce fields (CMS Enhanced at $185.4k ARR, no Analytics). Product JSON parsed from Financial response and upserted to `account_products` table. Meeting prep context includes product data. Meeting prep prompt can reason: "Customer has CMS Enhanced at $185.4k/year (annual), renewing Nov 2026. No current Analytics contract, open Parse.ly expansion in pipeline." Financial intelligence merges successfully regardless of product extraction success (best-effort bundling).
16. **Email enrichment optimization.** Briefing completes in <45 seconds. Email enrichment filters to 5-7 emails (not 20+), skips stale/duplicate entries, and runs non-blocking after briefing is written. Workflow re-run doesn't re-enrich emails enriched in last 24 hours. Timeout on email enrichment logs warning but doesn't block briefing delivery.

19. `pnpm tsc --noEmit` — clean.
