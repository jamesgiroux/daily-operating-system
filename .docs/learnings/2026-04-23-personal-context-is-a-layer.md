# Personal context is a layer

**Status:** stub. Drafting TBD.

## Synopsis

There are already systems of record for work, and there are increasingly strong systems for company-wide search. Neither of those owns the last mile of context that lives inside one person's day: what matters, what is slipping, what this account means to me, what I am carrying into the next meeting. This entry is about why DailyOS ended up defining that layer explicitly.

## Outline

- The lived moment: realising that Glean could know the company and Salesforce could know the account, but neither knew what I actually needed this morning.
- The three layers: system-of-record data, organizational context, and individual context.
- What belongs in the personal layer: interpretation, salience, local notes, user-authored facts, private working memory, and preparation state.
- Why this matters strategically: it explains local-first, the user entity, the Glean boundary, and why DailyOS should not try to become every other system.
- Why this matters organizationally: Customer Success and Key Accounts work is full of high-context interpretation that falls between products.
- What's still open: how much of this layer should remain strictly personal versus selectively publishable to a team.
