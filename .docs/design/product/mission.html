Why We Exist

Work has more information than ever, but very little of it feels personal.

Most tools know a slice of the truth. Your calendar knows where you are supposed to be. Email knows who is asking for what. Enterprise search knows what the organization can see. Salesforce, Linear, Gong, Slack, Docs, and notes each hold their own part of the story.

The person doing the work still has to assemble the meaning.

DailyOS exists to change that.

We believe intelligence should not only be powerful, shared, or searchable. It should be personal: grounded in your relationships, your commitments, your corrections, your priorities, and the context you need in the moment you need it.

The Mission

DailyOS makes intelligence personal.

DailyOS is memory plus judgment.

That means we build the layer that turns scattered work context into personal intelligence: timely, trustworthy, local, and useful before the user has to ask.

We are not trying to make people spend more time in software. We are trying to make them feel better prepared for the work they already have to do.

What Personal Intelligence Means

Personal intelligence is not a private copy of the company knowledge base. It is the user's working understanding of their world.

It knows:

  • who matters to this person
  • what has changed since last time
  • what they promised
  • what they corrected
  • which signals deserve attention
  • which context is relevant right now
  • what should be trusted, questioned, or verified

Organizational intelligence answers, "What does the company know?"

Personal intelligence answers, "What does this mean for me?"

What We Build

We build systems that keep individual work context current without turning the user into the system's operator.

The surfaces will change. Today they may look like briefings, meeting prep, account pages, person pages, action memory, forecasts, and impact capture. Tomorrow they may look different.

The mission is not the surface.

The mission is the personal intelligence underneath: the durable context layer that helps a person show up with memory, judgment, and readiness. Memory remembers what happened. Judgment helps decide what is current, trustworthy, relevant, and worth acting on.

What We Believe

The system should know before the user asks. If the context exists, DailyOS should connect it proactively.

The user's context belongs to the user. Personal intelligence should live on the user's machine, in systems and formats they can inspect, move, and leave with.

Trust is a product feature, not a disclaimer. Personal intelligence must show its work, remember corrections, respect uncertainty, and avoid pretending guesses are facts.

Preparation beats engagement. The goal is not more time in the app. The goal is a user who walks into important moments with the context they need.

Sharing starts with consent. Raw personal signals stay personal. Reports, summaries, and published artifacts can be shared when the user chooses to share them.

What We Refuse

We will not build another system the user has to maintain before it helps.

We will not treat personal context as organizational property.

We will not make the cloud the landlord of the user's professional memory.

We will not hide uncertainty behind confident prose.

We will not confuse a better briefing surface with the whole mission.

Decision Filter

When we are unsure what to build, ask:

  1. Does this make intelligence more personal, or just more automated?
  2. Does this give the user context when it matters, or more information to manage?
  3. Does this preserve ownership of personal context?
  4. Does this make the system more trustworthy in practice?
  5. Does this help the user show up better prepared for real work?

If the answer is no, it is probably not DailyOS.
